READING RESPONSE FOR ‘APPLE LISA’

“Perhaps that dichotomy was a sign of his design talent, as he was habituated to both problem solving and aesthetic values.” (89)
I found this introduction to Bill Atkinson to be quite interesting, as someone who had never heard of Atkinson or the Apple Lisa prior to
this reading. It’s not that common to find people that have duel careers in something as traditional as neurochemistry and as artistic as
photography/design. I connect to Atkinson in this way because I have a deep fascination with astronomy, as well as photography, design,
and fashion. Another thing I admire about Atkinson was his focus on the user interaction as a guide to the building the software. He
seemed to actually enjoy the process of trial and error, rebuilding code, numerous user tests, and even unsuccessful attempts at an idea.
I often settle on an idea and could benefit from applying more of Atkinson’s ideas in my own process.

I also found the passage about the Pull-Down Menus to be particularly interesting, since it followed the feature from it’s initial
conception as an idea, through the many iterations and trials, and finally on it’s success as a final product. Placing the menu on the top
of the screen rather then on the top of the window seems insignificant, but it’s decisions like that, that are thoroughly tested and agreed
upon, that makes all the difference for the user. The software should seem like a comprehensive language that only aids the user and you
can tell Atkinson followed through with this through many trials for his softwares. The reading was inspiring to someone like myself,
who constantly feels frustrated and overwhelmed by coding, that it’s all about trial and error. You can’t expect something to work
seamlessly the first time and once you accept that you will have to edit things, perform tests, and possibly even start over from scratch,
you free yourself of that burden of failure and open yourself up purely to new, ambitious ideas.
